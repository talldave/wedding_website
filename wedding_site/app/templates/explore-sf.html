Selected Parks

Here are just a few of San Franciscoâ€™s many parks.

Map of all listed parks


Dolores Park: All of San Francisco's neighborhoods and groups in one park. Grab a bite nearby and bring it to the park to enjoy. If you climb to the top, you get a great view of downtown.

Golden Gate Park: You can spend an entire day (or two) just in this park. Many of the roads are closed to traffic on the weekends leave it a perfect place to bike, run or walk. The De Young Museum and the California Academy of Science call the park home as does the Japanese Tea Garden and the Conservatory of Flowers.

The Presidio: A former military base, this park is slowly but surely being transformed. People still live, work and play here. You can get great views of the Golden Gate Bridge from Crissy Field and you will see lots of people who have rented bikes to bike over the bridge and back (or taking a ferry back from Sausalito).

Glen Park Canyon: This is a more rugged park, great for hiking and feeling like you have left the city behind.

Bernal Hill Park: You and a one hundred of the city's canine residents will enjoy a great 360 degree view of San Francisco and the surrounding area. On a clear day you can see both the Golden Gate and Bay bridges as well as Mount Tamalpais to in the north bay and Mount Diablo in the east bay.
