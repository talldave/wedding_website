Farm Tours

This is also farm country and driving around you will see lots of working farms. A few open their doors for tours and the following three farms cover your basics! The descriptions are adapted from their websites.

Map of listed farms


Tara Firma Farms

Saturday and Sunday at 10 am, noon and 2pm. No reservations required.

We invite you for a fun day enjoying the sun alongside happy piglets playing in the field and baby chicks that can fit into the palm of your hand. Take advantage of this wonderful opportunity to see a fully operational example of what sustainable farming looks like.

Green String Farm

Saturday at noon. No reservations required.

We love to share the work we do at Green String Farm with the community. See our grounds, hear about our farming practices, and learn about soil improvement and responsible land stewardship. We believe there is no border between Nature and agricultural space, and everyone can cultivate land the Green String way.

McClelland's Dairy

Time(s) TBD. Reservations required.

Enjoy an afternoon in Sonoma County on the McClelland’s Dairy. Learn about the history of the farm, pet baby calves, see where adult cows sleep when they’re not out eating lush pasture. Plus watch cows being milked in the parlor and milk a cow by hand! After the tour you may enjoy a sample of the European Style Organic Artisan butter. Before you leave, take a stroll through the Farm Store where products grown on the farm are sold. Reservations are required and the tour is about 1.5 hours in length.

Hiking

Again we are listing only a few possible hikes in the area. There is a strong likelyhood that one of these hikes will become a group activity.

Map of listed hikes/parks


London Lake Loop

This loop walk will include portions of the Beauty Ranch, the London’s experimental farm, vineyard views, a redwood forest walk, and London Lake. The walk will also take one to start-offs for several additional trails covering the bulk of Jack London SHP.

Difficulty: Easy
Length: 2.5 miles / 4.0 km
Duration: 1-3 hours
Family Friendly

Helen Putnam Regional Park Trails

Helen Putnam Regional Park lies just outside of Petaluma and offers a variety of terrain across 5.87 miles of trail. Excellent views of Petaluma to the east and rural Sonoma County to the west can be enjoyed from the Panorama Trail. The viewpoints may be accessed via a steep climb up the trail from the parking lot, or a gentler approach via the paved Ridge Trail past the Fish Pond. Fillaree, Pomo, and South Loop trails wind through forested areas offering shade on hot summer days. Equestrians, hikers, and bikers are welcome on all trails.

Length: 5.87 miles of trails
Parking: $7 per vehible for day use

Point Reyes

Pt Reyes Hostel to the Coastal Trail: There are many hiking trails to choose from in Point Reyes. This one brings you to coast from the Pt Reyes Hostel. Start your trip at the trailhead at the rear of the hostel lot. The trail starts as dirt road and turns into a two track before heading down hill for the last mile to the coastal trail. The coast camp is located just south on the coastal trail offering restrooms. Bring layers.

Difficulty: Moderate
Length: 10.0 miles / 16.1 km
Duration: half day
Family Friendly

Biking

You will see lots of people on bikes (keep an eye out for them while driving!). If you'd like to join them here are few routes for the fit and adventurous. It is likely that one of these hikes will become a group activity. The following rides and descriptions are from Bay Area Bike Rides Deck.

Map of listed bike ride starting points


Chileno Valley

This relative flat 47 mile ride start at Helen Putnam Regional Park, just outside of Petaluma, and goes through rolling, oak-covered hills on remote, lightly travelled roads where contented cows graze. Residents of rural Petaluma count on the dairy and poultry businesses for their livelihood. On Hick Valley Road is the one room Lincoln School, in service since 8172; attendance varies from 9 to 20 children. In the spring, Chileno Valley turns orange and yellow California poppies and other wild flowers. Marshall-Petalum Road, while not as well known as Chileno Valley Road, has some of the finest riding in the North Bay. This narrow two-lane country road heading through the coastal range sees almost no traffic. It offers hilltop views of Tomales Bay and winds through Verda Canyon alongside gently flowing creeks hidden by willows and the yellow-flowered Scotch broom. Many of the dairy farmers came from Switzerland generations ago and stayed. Don't miss downtown Petaluma, known for its Victorian buildings and where Francis Ford Coppola has films at least two movies.

Sonoma

Rolling hills of endless vineyards, secluded canyons, burbling creeks, and a redwood glen make this 42 mile journey worth the occasional steep grade. Complete the visit with a meal in Sonoma's historic town square, and try the wine. Although this route has some hills, a short casual ride can by taken by visiting just Glen Ellen. It can be hot and crowded in the summer; spring and fall months have the best weather. Traffic can be heavy on Sonoma Highway, so for the safest trip in the fairest weather, ride early.

Point Reyes

Point Reyes pennisula, separated from Fairfax by redwood-covered hills, gives cyclists a challenging 54 mile ride over rolling terrain near the Pacific coast's picturesque, rugged shoreline. The rural town for Olema on Highway 1 retains its country charm even with all of the growth in the San Francisco Bay Area over the past 40 years. The Cross Marin Recreational Path offers car free ride along tree-lined Lagunitas Creek. It's a spectacular sight in the fall when the leaves are turning.
